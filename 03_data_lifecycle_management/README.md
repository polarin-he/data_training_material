# POLARIN Data Lifecycle Management Training Material
## Repository Content
This folder outlines strategies for managing data throughout its entire lifecycle, from initial creation to long-term preservation and sharing. It includes materials on planning and implementing sustainable data stewardship practices.

### Key Topics
- **Planning: Creating and maintaining data management plans (DMPs) and data policies**
- **Practising: Strategies for sustainable data publication and preservation**

### Structure of repository
1. **Learning Modules**: Focused on each phase of the data lifecycle — from initial collection in extreme polar environments to long-term preservation and open data sharing.
2. **Case Studies**: Real-world examples from polar research projects, illustrating best practices in handling diverse data types.
3. **Additional Resources**: Links to relevant (polar) data management tools (template generators, compliance checkers, conversion tools), standards, and publications.

## Intended audience
This training material is meant for:

- Polar researchers and data curators within the POLARIN network
- Data managers and IT professionals in polar research institutions
- Policy makers and compliance officers in environmental and scientific data governance
- Anyone involved in (polar) research data collection, lifecycle management, or preservation

## The POLARIN project 
POLARIN is an international network of polar research infrastructures and their services, aiming at addressing the scientific challenges of the polar regions. 
The network includes a wide array of complementary and interdisciplinary top level research infrastructures: Arctic and Antarctic research stations, research vessels and icebreakers operating at both poles, observatories, data infrastructures and ice and sediment core repositories.

POLARIN will provide integrated, challenge-driven, and combined access to these infrastructures to facilitate interdisciplinary research on complex processes.
POLARIN has the following six specific objectives, coherent with the work packages, each with measurable outcomes:

- Enable science for understanding and predicting key processes in polar regions.
- Provide efficient challenge-driven transnational access (TA) to top level RIs in the polar regions.
- Improve data services and provide customised data products.
- Provide virtual access (VA) to data and data services.
- Provide training for infrastructure users.
- Advertise RI services and engage RI users. 

Want to learn more about the POLARIN project? Go to the [project website](https://eu-polarin.eu/).
